3. Conditional Gaussian Distribution

The conditional Gaussian distribution p(𝒙t-1 | 𝒙t, 𝒚) involves the mean and covariance depending on the conditioning variables 𝒙t and 𝒚.

Exercise 3: Explain what each term in the conditional Gaussian equation represents.

  • pθ(𝒙t-1 | 𝒙t, 𝒚): The conditional probability distribution of 𝒙t-1 given 𝒙t and 𝒚, parameterized by θ.
  • 𝒩(𝒙t-1; μθ(𝒙t, t, 𝒚), Σθ(𝒙t, t, 𝒚)): Indicates that 𝒙t-1 follows a Gaussian distribution with mean μθ(𝒙t, t, 𝒚) and covariance Σθ(𝒙t, t, 𝒚).
  • μθ(𝒙t, t, 𝒚): The mean of the Gaussian, which is a function of 𝒙t, time t, and conditioning variable 𝒚.
  • Σθ(𝒙t, t, 𝒚): The covariance matrix of the Gaussian, also a function of 𝒙t, time t, and 𝒚.

Problem 2: Explain the meaning of the covariance matrix Σ in the multivariate Gaussian distribution.

The covariance matrix Σ represents the variances and covariances of the components of the multivariate Gaussian distribution. The diagonal elements of Σ represent the variances of each individual component, while the off-diagonal elements represent the covariances between pairs of components. It determines the shape and orientation of the Gaussian distribution in multidimensional space.

Problem 3: Given p(xt-1 | xt, y) = \(\mathcal{N}(x_{t-1}; \mu(x_t, t, y), \Sigma(x_t, t, y))\), describe what would happen if Σ(xt, t, y) were a diagonal matrix.

If Σ(xt, t, y) were a diagonal matrix, it would indicate that the components of xt-1 are conditionally independent given xt and y. Each component's variance is determined independently, and there are no covariances between different components. The Gaussian distribution would be aligned with the coordinate axes.
